Rules To Follow

1. Dress Code: Visitors are required to dress modestly. Wearing traditional Indian attire is recommended, but if not possible, modest Western clothing is acceptable. Avoid wearing revealing clothes.

2. Footwear: Footwear is not allowed inside the temple complex. There are designated areas to deposit footwear before entering.

3. No Leather Items: Leather items such as belts, bags, wallets, and shoes are prohibited inside the temple premises.

4. Plastic Usage: Visitors are encouraged to minimize the use of plastic items within the temple complex. Avoid bringing single-use plastic bottles, bags, and containers. Opt for reusable alternatives or eco-friendly materials.

5. Mobile Phones and Cameras: Photography and use of mobile phones are strictly prohibited inside the temple.

6. Punctuality: Arrive early to avoid long queues and to ensure you have enough time for darshan (viewing of the deity).

7. Maintain Silence: Maintain silence inside the temple premises to ensure a peaceful environment for prayer and worship.

8. Respectful Behavior: Behave respectfully and follow the instructions of temple authorities and priests.

9. No Smoking or Drinking: Smoking, consumption of alcohol, and chewing tobacco are strictly prohibited inside the temple complex.

10. Avoid Eating Non-Vegetarian Food: It is advisable to avoid eating non-vegetarian food before visiting the temple.

11. Respect the Deity: Maintain a respectful distance from the deity and do not touch the idols or sacred objects without permission.

12. Follow Rituals: If you wish to participate in any rituals or ceremonies, follow the instructions given by the priests.

13. Donations: If you wish to make a donation, do so at the designated counters and avoid giving money or offerings directly to individuals.

14. Cleanliness: Keep the temple premises clean by disposing of trash in designated bins.

15. Security Checks: Be prepared for security checks at various entry points for the safety of visitors.

16. Exit Routes: Familiarize yourself with the exit routes in case of emergencies.
