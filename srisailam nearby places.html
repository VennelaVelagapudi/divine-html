Nearby Places To Visit

Sri Sakshi Ganapathi Swamy Temple

The Sri Sakshi Ganapathi Swamy Temple is a sacred place of worship dedicated to Lord Ganapathi, a beloved deity in Hinduism known as the remover of obstacles. Devotees visit this temple to seek blessings, guidance, and the removal of hurdles in their lives. The temple is revered for its serene atmosphere and its significance in Hindu religious practices.

Distance: 8 min (2.5 km) via SH 5e Rd

Akkamahadevi caves

The Akkamahadevi caves are ancient caves known for their historical and cultural significance. They are a place of pilgrimage and hold great spiritual importance for many people. These caves have been visited by pilgrims and tourists for centuries, and they continue to be a site of interest for those interested in history and religion.

Distance: 18 km

Srisailam Reservoir

Srisailam Reservoir, an expansive water body formed by a towering dam, invites visitors to explore its tranquil beauty amidst lush hills. Serving as a vital resource for the region, it sustains agriculture, supplies drinking water, and generates hydroelectric power. Boasting panoramic vistas and opportunities for boating and leisurely walks, the reservoir offers a serene retreat for travelers seeking solace in nature's embrace or adventure along its scenic shores.

Distance: 31 min (13.7 km) via SH 5 and NH 765

Phaladhara-Panchadhara

Phaladhara-Panchadhara is a revered site known for its natural beauty and spiritual significance. It comprises two streams, Phaladhara and Panchadhara, which are believed to possess sacred properties. People visit this place to experience its serene atmosphere and to take part in religious rituals. It holds a special place in the hearts of many devotees who seek solace and spiritual enlightenment in its tranquil surroundings.

Distance: 12 min (4.1 km) via SH 5
